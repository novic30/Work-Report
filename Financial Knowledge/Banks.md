Two main types of Banks are commercial and credit union banks.

Commercial Banks:
- Oldest banking form
- Widest range of services and locations
- Largest number of deposits
- publicly owned
- Eg Wells Fargo, CitiBank, CHASE

Credit Unions:
- Made to serve a specific community or organization like churches, large employer, labor union and government institutions.
- Have members which are like the shareholders
- Offer similar services as banks
- The money you deposit gets used to give loans and the interests from these loans is the money which keeps the credit union going.
- Using money from interest, they provides benefits and services to the customers

Interest: The amount of money paid to the depositor for leaving funds with the bank


ACCOUNTS:

Savings Account:
- Easy to access
- Can Take money in and out of the account easily
- Great place to put money aside as they're safe and easy deposits even without high return rates

Checking Account:
- Easy to open
- Used often to get paycheck deposited
- Provides you with tools, debit card, cheques, electronic transfers



CARDS:


Debit Card:
- Primary tool to access funds in accounts (checking or saving)
- Could be used to checkout at a register in a shop
- Could be used for withdrawals from ATMs (which might incur fees)



Fees from the banks:
- Banks and credit unions have a lot of different fees (some avoidable while others unavoidable).
- Fees could be for having an account, for processing transactions, for moving money, doing a variety of different things
- To minimize it and get lower/no fee, 
	- you should have all ur accounts and loans in the same banks or credit union
	- Having higher balances in checking account
	- Having payroll deposit in that bank / credit union


Overdrafts: when you write a check or do a
transaction with your debit card and you don't have enough money in your account to pay for it.

The bank pays / doesn't pay for it and would charge you a overcharge fee anyway. THIS IS MOST AVOIDABLE

Is your money safe at a bank or credit union? And the
short answer is yes. Both banks and credit unions have insurance that protects depositors in the
event of a failure of a bank or credit union
